Metric Reference Guide

Blood Glucose ▼ 70–100 mg/dL
Fasting blood sugar — strongest predictor (r = 0.42)
Diabetics average 194 mg/dL vs 132 in non-diabetics. Levels ≥ 200 mg/dL indicate diabetes. Prediabetes range: 126–199 mg/dL. Highest correlation with outcome in this dataset.
HbA1c ▼ < 5.7%
3-month average blood sugar — 2nd strongest predictor (r = 0.40)
Reflects glycated hemoglobin over ~90 days. Diabetics average 6.93% vs 5.39% in non-diabetics. Prediabetes: 5.7–6.4%. Diabetes: ≥ 6.5%.
BMI ▼ 18.5–24.9
Body Mass Index — obesity is a key modifiable risk factor
BMI ≥ 30 is classified as obese and significantly elevates diabetes risk. Dataset mean: 27.3. Range: 10–95.7. Correlation with diabetes: 0.21.
Hypertension ▼ < 120/80 mmHg
High blood pressure — shares many risk factors with diabetes
27.9% of hypertensive patients in this dataset have diabetes vs only 6.9% without. Often co-occurs due to shared metabolic dysfunction. Correlation: 0.20.
Heart Disease ▼ No prior history
Cardiovascular history — strongest binary risk indicator
32.1% of heart disease patients have diabetes — the highest association of any binary feature. Likely reflects shared atherosclerotic and insulin-resistance pathways.
Age ▼ Varies
Age is the 3rd strongest predictor (r = 0.26)
Risk increases substantially with age. Dataset range: 0.08–80 years, mean 41.9. Older adults show higher BMI, hypertension, and metabolic dysfunction rates.
